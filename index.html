Pinsent Masons self-reports to SRA after junior lawyer submits AI-hallucinated case law to High Court

A junior solicitor at Pinsent Masons used AI to draft two letters to the High Court in an insolvency matter both containing fabricated legal references. Judge Mullen found the firm had misled the court twice and the lawyer had almost entirely outsourced the thinking process to AI. The firm self-referred itself and three solicitors to the SRA making it the second top-tier UK firm to face SRA scrutiny over AI misuse this year.

Crime and Policing Act 2026 brings biggest shake-up to UK corporate criminal liability in a century

Coming into force on 29 June 2026 the Act extends the senior manager attribution model to all criminal offences not just economic crime. Previously companies could only be prosecuted where the offence was committed by someone at the very top. Now any senior manager acting within their authority can trigger corporate criminal liability meaning businesses must urgently review governance structures.
